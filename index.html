Dataset Overview

The ANUBIS dataset represents a significant advancement in skeletal action recognition benchmarking. Designed with a representation-centric approach, it addresses key limitations in existing datasets by providing comprehensive multi-viewpoint coverage and diverse action categories.

Our dataset captures 102 distinct action classes performed by 80 subjects across 80 synchronized viewpoints, resulting in over 66,000 video clips. The systematic inclusion of rear-view perspectives fills a critical gap in current benchmarks, enabling more robust evaluation of action recognition models.

Each action sequence is meticulously annotated with 32-joint 3D skeletal data, providing researchers with high-quality ground truth for developing and evaluating representation learning methods in skeletal action recognition.

Key Features

  • 80 synchronized viewpoints with systematic rear-view coverage
  • 32-joint 3D skeletal representation with precise temporal alignment
  • 102 action classes spanning social, aggressive, and independent behaviors
  • Professional recording environment with consistent lighting
  • Multi-person interaction scenarios with complex dynamics
  • Comprehensive evaluation protocols for representation learning

Action Categories

Comprehensive Action Taxonomy

The ANUBIS dataset features a carefully designed taxonomy that captures the full spectrum of human actions. Our categorization system distinguishes between independent single-person behaviors and complex multi-person interactions, with special attention to both social and aggressive behaviors.

This diverse collection enables comprehensive evaluation of action recognition models across different complexity levels and interaction types, making ANUBIS a valuable benchmark for advancing the field.
